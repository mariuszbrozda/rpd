Painting Advices !

What paint to choose for painting walls? Are you planning to paint your apartment or house? Changing the colour of the interior is associated with the selection of the right paint for painting the walls. On the market, the choice of paints for wall painting is enormous and can make you dizzy. When choosing paint in addition to colour, we should first of all consider its purpose. Not everyone will perform as well in the living room as in the kitchen. The paint is selected for the type of surface, ambient conditions and factors that will affect the coating. Paints should have three basic features: durability - the coating should be resistant to operational factors good adhesion to the ground easy to apply - suitable consistency, quick drying and strong coverage In addition, paint must be safe for people and the environment. Phthalic paints (alkyl) - create a strong, shiny or satin surface extremely resistant to cleaning and mechanical damage, but poorly permeate water vapor. They have high hiding power and grip, so you can cover them with stained walls without fear of being punctured. Perfectly suited for painting walls in the kitchen, bathrooms, corridors and stairwells.

Polyurethane paints - are used primarily for painting steel, wood, concrete and those made of light metals. They are very flexible and at the same time extremely resistant to abrasion. They are also recommended for painting bathrooms and kitchens. Vinyl paints - next to latex and acrylic, belong to the group of emulsion paints. They have very good covering properties, they are durable. They can be used on a variety of substrates - not only plastered walls or drywall, but also on wood. Silicate paints (silicate) - are more vapor-permeable than acrylic or vinyl paints and do not grow on mold. They create a wash-resistant coating with a deeply matt surface. They can be used in all rooms, especially in bathrooms. Structural and azure paints - combine the most important advantages of paints and plasters - they are high density paints, which when applied properly can create a decorative texture on the wall surface. Phthalic paints (alkyl) - create a strong, shiny or satin surface extremely resistant to cleaning and mechanical damage, but poorly permeate water vapor. They have high hiding power and grip, so you can cover them with stained walls without fear of being punctured. Perfectly suited for painting walls in the kitchen, bathrooms, corridors and stairwells. Undercoat paints - cheap acrylic, vinyl and latex paints. Pre-painted walls are painted with hard-to-remove stains, so the topcoat is better hidden and less worn.

Matt or satin? Currently matte finish is the most popular among painters. Matte paints are very easy to apply, create very elegant surfaces with a chalky appearance and their anti-reflective properties allow to hide the imperfections of the substrate. They can even be used on walls that are not perfectly smooth. Satin paints reflect a lot of light, perfectly suited to small and poorly lit rooms. Unfortunately, they emphasize all the imperfections of the wall That is why this type of paint is intended for painting on a perfectly prepared substrate. Remember also that the right quality and type of painting tools are no less important than choosing the right paint!
